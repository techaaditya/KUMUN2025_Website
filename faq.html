Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about KUMUN 2025

Model United Nations is an academic simulation where students learn about diplomacy, international relations, and the United Nations. Participants role-play as delegates from different countries and work to find solutions to real world problems.

KUMUN is open to all university and high school students. We welcome beginners and experienced delegates alike. No prior MUN experience is required.

The dress code for KUMUN is Western Business Attire. This includes suits, blazers, and formal footwear. Delegates are expected to dress professionally throughout the conference.

You can register through our online registration form. Click on the "Register Now" button and fill out all required information. Registration includes committee preference and payment details.

Registration fees vary by participant type. Please check the registration page for current pricing. Early bird discounts may be available for early registrations.

Information about accommodation options will be provided to registered delegates. The organizing committee can assist with recommendations for nearby hotels and lodging facilities.

Delegates should bring their country placard, research materials, writing supplies, and any electronic devices needed for note-taking. Business cards and professional attire are also required.

Research your assigned country's foreign policy, study the committee topics, practice parliamentary procedure, and prepare opening speeches. Background guides will be provided to help with preparation.

A position paper is a document that outlines your country's stance on the committee topics. It should include your country's policy, proposed solutions, and historical actions. Position papers are required for award consideration.

Awards are based on participation quality, diplomatic skills, research depth, and adherence to parliamentary procedure. Chairs evaluate delegates throughout the conference using standardized rubrics.

Yes, laptops and tablets are permitted for research and note-taking. However, phones should be on silent mode and used only for essential communication outside of sessions.

No problem! KUMUN welcomes beginners. We provide comprehensive training workshops before the conference, and experienced delegates and chairs are always willing to help newcomers.

Consider your interests, academic background, and the topics being discussed. Read the committee descriptions and background guides to make an informed decision. You can indicate multiple preferences during registration.

English is the official language of KUMUN 2025. All speeches, documents, and communications will be conducted in English.

Yes, lunch is provided on all conference days, along with morning and afternoon refreshments. Special dietary requirements can be accommodated with advance notice.

Cancellation policies vary by registration period. Early cancellations may be eligible for partial refunds. Please contact the organizing committee for specific details.

You can reach us at kumun@ku.edu.np or call our team members directly. Contact information is available on the website and in the footer section.

Yes, KUMUN includes social events and networking opportunities. These provide delegates with chances to build connections and relax between sessions.

Observers can attend committee sessions to learn about MUN procedures and watch debates. They do not participate in discussions but can gain valuable experience for future participation.

Position papers should be submitted via email to the respective committee chairs by September 1, 2025. Format guidelines will be provided in the background guides.

Public buses run regularly from Kathmandu to Dhulikhel. Private vehicles and taxis are also available. The organizing committee may arrange shuttle services for registered delegates.

No, delegates are assigned to one committee for the duration of the conference. This allows for deeper engagement and more meaningful participation in committee discussions.
