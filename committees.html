Our Committees

Download background guides and read committee agendas carefully before attending delegate training

United Nations Security Council (UNSC)

The UNSC is where the world's most pressing peace and security challenges are debated. From conflict zones to global threats, delegates here take on the responsibility of making decisions that can shape the fate of nations.

United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC)

The UNHRC stands as the global forum for protecting and promoting human rights. Delegates address violations, develop recommendations, and work toward a world where dignity and equality are universal.

United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR)

The UNHCR focuses on the rights and protection of refugees, displaced people, and stateless communities. Delegates here work on practical solutions to provide safety, shelter, and hope to those forced from their homes.

United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP)

The UNEP is dedicated to protecting the planet's natural resources. Delegates address issues like climate change, biodiversity loss, and pollution, aiming to create a sustainable future for all.

Federal Parliament of Nepal (FPN)

The FPN simulates Nepal's legislative process, giving delegates the chance to debate national policies, propose laws, and address pressing issues affecting the country's governance and development.

International Press (IP)

The IP captures the conference in motion. Journalists, photographers, and media delegates report on debates, conduct interviews, and produce stories that bring the MUN to life for everyone.

Economic and Financial Committee (EcoFin)

EcoFin deals with the global economy, trade, and development. Delegates here work to address economic crises, fair trade practices, and financial stability in an interconnected world.

International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA)

The IAEA promotes the peaceful use of nuclear energy while preventing its misuse. Delegates address nuclear safety, energy policies, and ways to prevent nuclear weapons proliferation.

Committee Topics & Agendas

UNSC - Security Council

Topic A: Addressing the Crisis in the Middle East

Examining regional conflicts, humanitarian crises, and peace-building efforts in the Middle East region.

Topic B: Cybersecurity Threats to International Peace

Addressing cyber warfare, digital espionage, and the regulation of emerging technologies in conflict zones.

UNHRC - Human Rights Council

Topic A: Protecting Freedom of Expression in the Digital Age

Balancing free speech with preventing online harassment, misinformation, and hate speech.

Topic B: Rights of Indigenous Peoples and Land Disputes

Addressing land rights, cultural preservation, and economic development for indigenous communities.

UNHCR - Refugee Agency

Topic A: Climate-Induced Displacement

Addressing the growing crisis of people displaced by climate change and environmental disasters.

Topic B: Integration of Refugees in Host Communities

Developing policies for successful refugee integration, education, and economic participation.

UNEP - Environment Programme

Topic A: Plastic Pollution in Marine Ecosystems

Developing international strategies to reduce plastic waste and protect ocean biodiversity.

Topic B: Renewable Energy Transition in Developing Nations

Supporting sustainable energy development while ensuring energy access for all communities.

FPN - Federal Parliament of Nepal

Topic A: Federalism and Local Governance

Strengthening federal structure and empowering local governments in Nepal.

Topic B: Economic Recovery Post-Pandemic

Developing policies for sustainable economic growth and job creation in Nepal.

IP - International Press

Role: Conference Coverage

Journalists will cover all committee sessions, conduct interviews, and produce daily newsletters.

Special Focus: Investigative Reporting

In-depth coverage of key debates, behind-the-scenes stories, and delegate profiles.

EcoFin - Economic Committee

Topic A: Digital Currency Regulation

Establishing international frameworks for cryptocurrency and digital payment systems.

Topic B: Global Supply Chain Resilience

Addressing supply chain disruptions and building economic resilience post-pandemic.

IAEA - Atomic Energy Agency

Topic A: Nuclear Energy Safety Standards

Updating international safety protocols for nuclear power plants and waste management.

Topic B: Nuclear Technology for Peaceful Development

Promoting nuclear applications in medicine, agriculture, and sustainable development.

Background Guides & Preparation

Background guides provide essential information for delegates to prepare for their committee sessions. Each guide includes topic analysis, country positions, and suggested research areas.

What's in a Background Guide?

  • Topic overview and historical context
  • Current international situation
  • Key stakeholders and their positions
  • Previous UN resolutions and actions
  • Suggested research questions
  • Bibliography and resources

Position Paper Guidelines

  • Maximum 2 pages per topic
  • Country's stance on the issue
  • Proposed solutions and policies
  • Historical actions taken by the country
  • Submission deadline: September 1, 2025
  • Required for award consideration

Research Tips

  • Study your country's foreign policy
  • Research recent UN resolutions
  • Understand regional alliances
  • Prepare opening speeches
  • Identify potential allies
  • Practice parliamentary procedure
